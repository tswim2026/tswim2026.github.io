Innovating with Care: Building Effective and Responsible Human–AI Service Systems

Organizations are rapidly developing AI-augmented services, fundamentally changing how we deliver and experience services. While we value innovation, the explosion of AI integration raises profound questions about responsibility, equity, and trust. While we value innovation, the explosion of AI integration raises profound questions about responsibility, equity, and trust. Recognizing the perils of unintended consequences, we seek research promoting innovation with care, that not only advances technological frontiers but also integrates ethical reflection and social responsibility. Together, we set our sights on information systems scholarship that ensures that AI fulfills its potential for our collective good. [read more]

Session Formats

Presentation Session

Session for polished manuscripts targeting leading journals in Information Systems. Each paper will receive generous time for presentation and discussion, creating space for serious scholarly exchange. Feedback targets theoretical contribution, framing choices, and anticipated reviewer reactions.

Best suited for: Authors who want deep, candid feedback from senior scholars at a critical stage of revision.

Research Incubator Session

A small-group, developmental session in an Interactive Forum format. Authors briefly present their core idea and identify bottlenecks. Senior scholars serve as strategic advisors focused on unblocking research—clarifying next steps, sharpening theoretical lenses, and strengthening project trajectories.

Best suited for: Projects with compelling phenomena or early insights facing walls in theory building, framing, or design.

Why Submit to TSWIM?

Non-Archival Workshop

Papers are NOT published in proceedings, ensuring you retain full copyright to submit to journals later.

Editors as Mentors

Participants engage directly with Editors who act as mentors, not just evaluators.

Low-Pressure, High-Trust Environment

Scholars gain constructive guidance, fresh perspectives, and opportunities for collaboration without reputational risk.

Submission Guidelines

  • Any submission must name a coauthor presenter who is a faculty member or a doctoral student.
  • English is the language of the conference and all submissions.
  1. It is suggested to download the template file and paste the content of your paper into the template file.
  2. Completed research papers are up to 15 pages in English version.
  3. Short/working papers are up to 8 pages in English version.
  4. The submissions must be kept anonymous for review. Hence, please do not reveal author identities in the submissions.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 27 May 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the TSWIM 2026 workshop handles personal information collected through this website. We are committed to protecting your privacy and processing your data lawfully, including in accordance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) for visitors in the European Economic Area.

1. Who we are

TSWIM 2026 is hosted by the Institute of Service Science, National Tsing Hua University (NTHU), Hsinchu, Taiwan, which acts as the data controller for personal data collected via this site. For any privacy-related questions or requests, contact us at tswim2026@iss.nthu.edu.tw.

2. What we collect

We only collect the information you voluntarily provide when you register for the workshop. Depending on the registration form, this may include:

  • Your name (English and, optionally, Chinese)
  • Email address and phone number
  • Affiliation and department
  • Attendee role and participation type (e.g. TSWIM and/or NSTC workshop)
  • Banquet attendance, shuttle, and meal preferences (including dietary needs you choose to share)
  • Accessibility requirements or other notes you choose to provide
  • NTHU parking voucher request
  • Receipt title and tax ID, where you request an invoice

3. How we use it and our legal basis

We use this information solely to administer your participation in TSWIM 2026 — to process your registration and payment, plan catering and logistics, issue receipts, and communicate with you about the event. The legal basis is the performance of your registration request and our legitimate interest in organising the workshop; where you provide dietary or accessibility information, this is used only to accommodate your needs.

4. Who processes your data

Registration data submitted through this website is transmitted to and stored using Google Workspace services (Google Apps Script and Google Sheets/Drive), which act as our data processor. Payment is handled by our payment provider, ECPay, which processes your transaction details under its own privacy terms. We do not sell or share your personal data with any other third parties, except where required by law.

5. Cookies and tracking

This website does not use cookies, and it does not run any analytics or advertising trackers. We do not store any information on your device, and we do not build visitor profiles. Web fonts are served directly from this website (self-hosted), so no font or tracking requests are sent to Google when you browse the site.

6. External links

Some pages link to external services such as Google Maps (for venue and hotel directions) and link-shortening services. These are only contacted if you click the link, at which point the respective provider's privacy policy applies.

7. Data retention

We keep your registration data only for as long as needed to administer the workshop and to meet any applicable accounting or legal obligations.

8. Your rights

You may request access to, correction of, or deletion of your personal data, and you may object to or request restriction of its processing. To exercise these rights, email us at tswim2026@iss.nthu.edu.tw. If you are in the EEA, you also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with a revised "last updated" date.
