Taiwan Summer Workshop on Information Management

National Tsing Hua University

Hsinchu, Taiwan
Jun 29 - July 01, 2026

Reasons to Submit and/or Attend

Review Stage:

  1. To obtain constructive review suggestions from scholars who have published in very good journals

On-site Stage (for presenter):

  1. To obtain on-site feedback with editors and top scholars before submitting to top journals
  2. To have ample time to interact with editors and top scholars
  3. To obtain inspiration for new research

On-site Stage (for participant):

  1. To learn from quality presentations and comments by editors and top scholars
  2. To make friends with international and Taiwanese scholars, enabling possible collaboration

After TSWIM:

  1. Free to proceed to journal submission without duplicate publication issues
  2. Upon authors' agreement, high quality paper will be recommended to PAJAIS (Pacific Asia Journal of the Association for Information Systems) for fast-track review

Submission to this TSWIM indicates that the authors understand:

  1. This TSWIM does not require copyright from authors. By submitting to TSWIM, the authors granted TSWIM to have their names and paper titles appear on the conference webpage.
  2. This TSWIM will not publish abstract nor the main text. This arrangement facilitates future submissions to journals, after TSWIM.
  3. This TSWIM particularly welcomes submissions that are aimed to be published in "Senior Scholars' List of Premier Journals" of the Association for Information Systems (AIS) (https://aisnet.org/page/SeniorScholarListofPremierJournals).

Call for Papers

We welcome submissions aligned with the conference theme. Suggested topics include, but are not limited to:

  • AI and New Technologies for Humans
  • Artificial Intelligence in Business and Society
  • Artificial Intelligence and Autonomous Applications
  • Human Behavior and IS
  • Human Computer Interaction
  • IS Innovation, Adoption and Diffusion
  • IS Leadership, Strategy, and Governance
  • IT Innovation Behavior and Entrepreneurship
  • Big Data for Public Safety and Personal Security
  • Business Analytics Management and Applications
  • Business Models in the Digital World
  • Digital Governance: Challenges and Opportunities
  • Digital Innovation, Transformation and Entrepreneurship
  • Electronic Commerce and Mobile Commerce
  • Financial Technology (FinTech), Blockchain and the Internet of Value
  • Financialization, Finance and Information Technology
  • Information and Communication Technologies for Development
  • Information Systems Security and Privacy
  • Accounting Information Systems and Digitization
  • IT/IS in Healthcare

Submission Guidelines

  • Any submission must name a coauthor presenter who is a faculty member or a doctoral student.
  • English is the language of the conference and all submissions.
Submission Download TSWIM2026 Submission Template
  1. It is suggested to download the template file and paste the content of your paper into the template file.
  2. Completed research papers are up to 15 pages in English version.
  3. Short/working papers are up to 8 pages in English version.
  4. The submissions must be kept anonymous for review. Hence, please do not reveal author identities in the submissions.
